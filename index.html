Sexual Harassement

Sexual harassment remains a silent epidemic across Nigerian universities, where countless students, mostly women: are coerced, intimidated, or abused by those in power. Fear of backlash, social stigma, and institutional neglect keep victims quiet while perpetrators continue unchecked, deepening a culture of silence and impunity

Weak Reporting System

Most Nigerian universities lack safe, reliable channels for students to report abuse. Victims often face broken processes, delayed responses, or outright cover-ups. Without transparent systems in place, trust is lost, data is hidden, and justice becomes a privilege, rather than a right, for the few who speak up.

Accountability

At EduEthics, we hold ourselves responsible for every action we take as a team. From following up on reported cases to documenting our processes with transparency, accountability is the foundation of how we build trust, both within our team and with every user we serve.
